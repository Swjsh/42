### Reason the candidate hypothesis was structurally sound but the implementation failed

The directional insight is correct: flat VIX at elevated levels (>17.30) is NOT a bear-entry
contra-indicator. The problem is NOT the filter logic — it's the entry timing. Unlocking F8
on flat-VIX bars allows the engine to fire on ANY flat-VIX bar that passes all other filters,
including bars BEFORE J's actual setup fires. The engine creates a "noise filter F8 was suppressing."

**The root cause:** F8 was functioning as an ACCIDENTAL noise filter. Rising VIX is temporarily
correlated with the specific bar-structure that produces J's winning entries (the momentum move
that causes VIX to tick up AND creates the bear ribbon + level rejection simultaneously). Removing
this correlation filter lets in more early-entry noise even though the logic is "correct."

**Alternative approach (lower priority, needs evidence):** Rather than changing F8, investigate
whether the combination of `bar_idx_in_session > N` (wait for first 30 min to settle) AND
`vix_now > 17.50` on a flat day would avoid the early-entry noise while still capturing the 5/19 move.
This would require a targeted `min_bars_before_entry` parameter, not an F8 change.

## Original DRAFT (preserved for reference)

## Hypothesis

F8 currently requires `vix_direction == "rising"` (strict) to pass. The `vix_direction()`
function uses a ±0.05 deadband — any reading within that window returns "flat." Since "flat"
is not "rising," flat VIX bars are silently blocked by F8 even though VIX being flat signals
no volatility regime change (it is actively stable, not collapsing).

**On 2026-05-19** — the triggering observation — ALL 76 RTH bars were classified "flat"
(VIX ranged 18.02→17.80, max decline 0.44 pts, mean per-bar decline 0.10 pts from 5-bar high).
Zero bars were "rising." F8 blocked every bar except the 17 where VIX happened to tick up
by more than 0.05 in a single 5m bar. SPY fell $3.07 from 735.40 → 732.33 — a textbook
bear session the engine mostly missed.

**The directional claim:** Allowing flat VIX (change F8 from `vd == "rising"` to
`vd in {"rising", "flat"}`) would capture bear entries on sessions where VIX is elevated
but rangebound — which is a valid bear environment, not a volatility-collapse warning.
Adding a higher VIX floor (17.50 vs 17.30) when VIX is flat provides a discrimination buffer
against borderline MID-regime sessions.

**Proposed rule change (targeted):**

```python
# CURRENT (production):
vix_pass = ctx.vix_now > VIX_BEAR_THRESHOLD and vd == "rising"

# PROPOSED (flat-VIX unblock with higher floor):
VIX_FLAT_BEAR_THRESHOLD = 17.50  # higher than 17.30 to guard MID-regime borderline days
vix_pass = (
    (ctx.vix_now > VIX_BEAR_THRESHOLD and vd == "rising")  # original rising path
    or (ctx.vix_now > VIX_FLAT_BEAR_THRESHOLD and vd == "flat")  # new flat path with higher floor
)
# "falling" still always blocks — genuine VIX collapse is a real warning.
```

This is NOT a general relaxation of F8. It is a targeted fix for a specific mis-classification:
flat VIX treated as a bear-entry block when it should be neutral.

## Root cause captured

The investigation also revealed a structural insight about F8's current production behavior that
should be logged regardless of whether this candidate is ratified:

**F8 blocks 13,328 bars as "flat" (not rising) out of 13,854 total F8-blocked bars (96%).
Only 526 bars (4%) are blocked for truly "falling" VIX.** The filter's name in the codebase
says "VIX > 17.30 AND vix_rising" but the practical effect is "VIX > 17.30 AND vix_rising AND
NOT flat" — which means a session with perfectly stable elevated VIX (the ideal scenario for
bear bias) is treated identically to a collapsing-VIX session. This is a regime-sensitivity
mis-specification, not just a threshold calibration issue.

**What flat VIX actually means:** VIX is stable — neither spiking (market fear rising) nor
collapsing (market fear resolving). In elevated VIX regimes (VIX > 17.50), stable VIX
means the fear premium is sustained without fresh sellers exiting — a neutral-to-bearish
condition for SPY. The current F8 treats it as a contra-indicator. That's backwards.
