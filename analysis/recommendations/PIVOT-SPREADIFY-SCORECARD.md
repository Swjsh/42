### VERDICT: NO_IMPROVEMENT  (debit spread does NOT rescue the drawdown)

**The thesis is FALSIFIED on this data.** A debit vertical does NOT reduce the book
drawdown of the live vwap_continuation edge. Hard numbers (real OPRA fills, QTY=3,
same OPRA loader for both legs so fills are byte-consistent):

- **No spread cell improves BOTH book maxDD and recency total while OOS-positive.** Zero
  of 54 cells. Every recency-rescuing cell has WORSE (more negative) book maxDD than its
  same-tier long baseline.
- The auto-picked "best" cell (long ATM / short +$4 OTM / pt=0.5 / stop=0.99) posts a
  higher full exp/tr ($96.14 vs $48.33) and far bigger recent total ($2232 vs $408) — but
  its book maxDD is $-829.8 vs the long baseline's $-454.56 (WORSE by $375), and its
  per-trade Sortino is 0.31 vs the long baseline's 0.88 (WORSE).

**Why (the honest mechanism — C3):** the long single-leg's small maxDD ($-454/$-612) is a
DIRECT consequence of the -8% premium stop — it caps every loss at ~$39/trade (3 lots).
That tight stop is what whipsaws in chop (16/25 recent losses exit EXIT_ALL_PREMIUM_STOP,
WR 36%), but it ALSO keeps the equity curve shallow. The spread cells that "rescue"
recency win by setting stop=0.99/None (i.e. REMOVING the tight stop) + width=$4 — they let
winners run, but losers then run to near-full debit (~$326/trade for 3 lots), enlarging the
drawdown. The recovery is "no tight stop + ride wide," NOT "the spread structure caps
risk." A debit vertical's defined-risk floor ($326/3-lot) only binds below the -8% stop's
floor ($39) — so against THIS tightly-stopped baseline the spread can only ADD downside.

**Capital-normalization caveat (the one point in the spread's favor):** per-trade
buying-power-at-risk is much lower for the spread — net debit ~$109/lot ($326/3-lot max
loss) vs the single-leg premium ~$491/3-lot (max $2646 on a wide-range day). If the
constraint were BUYING POWER (small account, many concurrent positions) rather than per-$
equity variance, the spread lets you hold the edge with a hard-capped, far smaller and more
predictable cost basis. But that is a sizing/BP argument, not a drawdown-reduction argument:
on a $-for-$ equity-curve basis (what book maxDD measures), the spread is strictly worse here.

**Bottom line for #1:** the live edge is ALREADY survivable in the chop regime via its tight
premium stop — recent windows are degraded (exp/tr ATM $48->$16, ITM-2 $78->$27, WR 36%)
but still NET POSITIVE (+$408 / +$666), not RED. The spread does not make it MORE survivable;
it trades a shallow-stopped curve for a deeper-swinging one. **Do not ship.** Keep the
long single-leg with the -8% stop. If recency degradation is the worry, the lever is the
ENTRY/regime filter or stop calibration (C28: exit tuning has diminishing returns once
stop-rate is high), not a debit spread.
