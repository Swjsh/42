### 6. Lesson of the day

When the premarket high has already exceeded a key resistance level before RTH open, the "first-touch rejection" thesis at that level is invalid — the pre-market sweep is evidence that the level was already tested and absorbed, making a continuation move more likely than a rejection on first RTH touch. Going forward, the premarket audit step should explicitly check: if PMH > key_resistance, downgrade the rejection probability and note that the open-drive directional thesis takes precedence. This is a calibration fix, not a new rule — the next time a post-catalyst Friday produces a PMH that briefly exceeds the prior RTH high, the prediction should read "breakout continuation if SPY holds above 736.11 in first 15 min" rather than "rejection at 736.11."
