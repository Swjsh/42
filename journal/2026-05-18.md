### 6. Lesson of the day

The ribbon (Saty Pivot Ribbon) is a retest-quality gate, not a first-strike trigger. When a named level (738.10 Carry, 738.86 flipped support) breaks on high volume, the setup IS the break — the signal is price closing below the level with confirming volume. The ribbon will typically confirm the direction 3–5 bars later, by which time the move is already 70–80% complete. Today's 3 blocked bear setups all scored ≥8/10 on everything except ribbon stack ordering, and all 3 would have been winners. The fix is not to remove the ribbon filter entirely — it genuinely protects against false retests in choppy conditions — but to create a `broken_level_retest` path that bypasses the ribbon-stack requirement when: (1) a named ★★+ level has broken (5m bar closed >10¢ past it), and (2) a subsequent bar retests the level from the broken side and closes back through with body ≥20¢ and volume ≥1.5×. The level rejection IS the trigger; ribbon confirmation is for the retest quality, not the first-break entry.
