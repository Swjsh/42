## Trades

### Trade #1 — Descending Trendline Rejection (Short Puts on 0DTE) — *draft setup*

- **Direction / contract:** SPY 2026-05-01 $721 **PUT** (0DTE).
- **Bias on the day:** Bearish. SPY ran to morning high (~724.80 at 10:00), formed a clear descending trendline through subsequent lower highs. EMA ribbon turned bearish (red) by ~11:30. Multiple yellow sell-signal triangles on indicator before 13:00. Structurally a sell day after the morning failure.
- **Pre-trade thesis (reconstructed):** Anticipating that SPY would re-test the descending trendline from below and reject — buy 0DTE 721 puts to capture the next leg lower toward 720 / 719 horizontal levels.
- **Entry leg #1 — PREMATURE (the mistake):**
  - Filled 13:09:14 EDT, 10 contracts @ $0.46. SPY ~722.20.
  - SPY had **not yet** re-tested the trendline. The trigger had not fired. This was an **anticipation entry**, not a signal entry.
  - $460 deployed.
- **Adverse move:** SPY rallied up toward the trendline. Premium dropped to $0.19 by 13:36 (−59%). This adverse move was *expected by the actual setup* — the trendline test is part of the trigger — but punished J's premature entry.
- **Entry leg #2 — THE REAL TRIGGER:**
  - Filled 13:36:11 EDT, 10 contracts @ $0.19. SPY ~723.20 (kissed the descending trendline, got rejected).
  - This is when the trigger actually fired: descending-trendline test + rejection.
  - The premium being $0.19 (vs. the original $0.46) was incidental — the *signal* is what justified the entry, not the discount.
  - $190 deployed. Total position: 20 contracts, blended cost $0.325.
- **Management:** No stop was documented pre-entry. Going forward this is a hard requirement; the trade ran without an explicit kill level.
- **Exit:**
  - Filled 14:47:55 EDT, sold all 20 @ $0.56. SPY ~722.
  - Took profit into the next intraday support / mid-range. Disciplined exit at a defined level rather than holding for the bigger move.
- **What happened after exit:** SPY continued lower into the close — 720.01, then 719.80, with a "Death Cross" indicator marker around 16:00 and a heavy-volume sell candle. The 721 puts almost certainly went deeper ITM into the close. **Money was left on the table** — not necessarily wrong (target/exit was hit), but worth noting.
- **P&L:** +$470 / +72.3% on $650 capital deployed. **R-multiple: undefined** (no documented stop).

### What J got right
- **Bias was correct.** Bearish read of structure (descending trendline + bearish ribbon + sell signals) was vindicated by price action.
- **Second entry was a real signal.** Trendline test + rejection at 13:36 is a legitimate, repeatable trigger.
- **Exit was disciplined.** Took profit into a defined intraday level rather than holding for "more."

### What J got wrong (J's own diagnosis, confirmed)
- **First entry was premature.** Bias ≠ trigger. The setup hadn't fired yet at 13:09.
- **No documented stop.** This trade ran without a kill level. It worked; the next one might not.
- **Position math wasn't run.** $650 deployed on a sub-$5K account is significant; the per-trade risk and premium-as-%-of-equity numbers weren't computed pre-entry.

### The repeatable lesson
**Wait for the trigger.** The trendline-rejection setup is real. The mistake was entering before the trigger fired. The fix is to *not* take the anticipation entry, and instead wait for the actual rejection candle (or two-candle confirmation) at the trendline.

If we'd waited and only taken the 13:36 entry at $0.19 (10 contracts only): cost $190, exit $560, profit **+$370 on $190 = +194%**. Bigger return, smaller risk, cleaner process. That's the trade we want to repeat.
