### Where the predictions held
1. **723.65 was the pivot.** It capped the morning rally and acted as the line in the sand. Marking it correctly meant we had context for every move.
2. **VIX 17.30 was the right threshold.** The one moment VIX cleared it (17.31 at 2:41) was the bears' best moment all day.
3. **Volume divergence rule.** The 1:05 wick was correctly invalidated by the 42K recovery bar.
4. **725.00 was the next resistance up.** SPY tagged 725.04 — within 4 cents of the marked level.

### Where the predictions missed
1. **No bullish playbook.** We saw the breakout at 1:50 PM, recognized it for what it was, and watched it run with no plan to participate. Two 70K+ volume bars in one afternoon is a tradeable pattern that we couldn't act on. The cost today wasn't a loss — it was opportunity left on the table because the playbook is one-sided.
2. **Multi-day downtrend ≠ today's tape.** The bearish lean from the daily chart was correct context, but the intraday 5-min tape flipped bullish at 1:50 PM and never looked back. Holding the bearish thesis mentally for an hour after the breakout is the kind of error that turns into chasing puts at the wrong level. Filters saved us.
3. **VIX direction prediction.** Expected VIX to rise on any pullback. Reality: VIX fell all afternoon (17.30 → 17.20). When VIX is falling, puts are dead regardless of price action.

### Surprise events
- **Two 70K+ volume bullish bars** in a single afternoon. The second one (3:10 PM, 75K vol) was the biggest of the day. This is breakout signature, not chop signature.
- **Slow EMA crossing 724.00** at 3:10 PM. The whole ribbon lifted above the morning resistance. Structurally, that turns 724 from "resistance to fade" into "support to defend."
- **Ribbon never flipped, even in the 2:30–2:41 window.** The Fast EMA only ticked down 1 cent on the entire pullback. That's how strong the underlying bid was.

### Lesson of the day

**One-sided playbooks miss days like today.** We had bearish filters running for ~3 hours. Three of five filters cleared at 2:41 PM. None ever cleared simultaneously because the ribbon stayed bullish. The discipline held — and the discipline was correct because puts would have lost.

But the bullish mirror setup was firing on the same chart with the same ribbon, and we couldn't trade it. The lesson isn't "trade more aggressively" — it's **the playbook needs both sides to make the rig complete**. The filters work in either direction. The infrastructure works. The missing piece is just the named pattern with rules. J needs to provide call trade examples and we need to draft BULLISH_RECLAIM_RIDE_THE_RIBBON formally.
